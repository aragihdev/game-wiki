Chara

The First Human and the Fallen Child

Chara is the first human to ever fall into the Underground. While they are not the protagonist, their presence is felt throughout the entire game, as they are the character the player originally names at the start of the journey.

Physical Characteristics: Very similar in appearance to Frisk, Chara wears a green sweater with a single yellow stripe, brown pants, and shoes. Unlike Frisk, they have rosy cheeks, wide open eyes, and a permanent, somewhat eerie smile.

Personality

Chara's true personality is a subject of much debate, as it is mostly revealed through the memories of other monsters:

  • Complex Bond: Asriel recalls that Chara "wasn't the greatest person" and harbored a deep hatred for humanity, though they became like a sibling to him and a child to the Dreemurrs.
  • Determined: Even as a child, Chara was incredibly driven, eventually hatching a dark plan to sacrifice themselves to free the monsters.
  • The Narrator? Many fans believe that Chara acts as the narrator of the game, their voice becoming more cynical and direct as the player gains LOVE (Level of Violence).

Abilities and Powers

In life, Chara was a normal human, but in death, they become a manifestation of the player's statistics:

  • Essence of Power: Chara describes themselves as "the feeling you get when a number increases"—the HP, ATK, and GOLD gained from killing.
  • Timeline Destruction: At the end of a Genocide Route, Chara possesses enough power to erase the entire world with a single strike, dealing an astronomical amount of damage (999,999...).
  • Soul Ownership: If the player wishes to restore the world after a Genocide Route, Chara demands the player's soul in exchange, permanently altering all future Pacifist endings.

History

Chara fell into the Underground long before Frisk, around the year 201X.

  • Adopted by Royalty: They were found by Asriel and taken to the palace, where they were loved as a member of the family.
  • The Plan: Chara intentionally poisoned themselves with buttercup flowers so that Asriel could absorb their soul, cross the barrier, and collect six more human souls to free everyone.
  • The Failure: The plan failed when Asriel refused to fight the humans, leading to both of their deaths. Chara's body was eventually buried beneath the bed of golden flowers where Frisk first falls.

Trivia

  • Name Origin: The name "Chara" is likely short for "Character." If the player enters "Chara" as the name of the fallen child, the game responds with: "The true name."
  • Locket and Knife: The "Heart Locket" and the "Real Knife" (or "Worn Dagger") found in New Home originally belonged to them.
  • Chocolate: If the player checks the fridge in Asgore's house during a Genocide Route, red text appears saying "No chocolate," implying it was Chara's favorite treat.
