Asriel Dreemurr

The Prince of the Underground and God of Hyperdeath

Asriel is the biological son of Asgore and Toriel and the true identity of Flowey. He serves as the final boss of the True Pacifist Route, representing the emotional core of the game's narrative regarding mercy, loss, and redemption.

Physical Characteristics: As a child, he is a small, white boss monster wearing a green sweater with yellow stripes. In his "God of Hyperdeath" form, he becomes a towering figure with black facial markings, large horns, and a long robe featuring the Delta Rune.

Personality

Asriel's personality varies drastically depending on his form and the presence of a soul:

  • The Gentle Soul: As a child, Asriel was known for being a "crybaby," extremely kind, and deeply loyal to his adoptive sibling, Chara.
  • The Nihilist: Without a soul (as Flowey), he became incapable of feeling love, leading to a cycle of cruelty.
  • The God of Hyperdeath: During the final battle, he projects a persona of absolute power and cynicism, though this is revealed to be a facade for his immense loneliness and desire to see his friend again.

Abilities and Powers

After absorbing nearly every soul in the Underground, Asriel achieves a god-like status with infinite stats:

  • Chaos Saber & Chaos Buster: He wields twin glowing sabers and a powerful energy cannon that fires rainbow-colored blasts.
  • Shocker Breaker: He summons lightning bolts that strike the battlefield in rapid succession.
  • Hyper Goner: A massive, skull-like device that attempts to consume the entire timeline, forcing the protagonist to struggle for survival.
  • Angel of Death: His ultimate form where he becomes invulnerable, forcing the protagonist to "SAVE" their friends' souls to reach him.

History

Asriel's story is the central tragedy of the Underground.

  • The Sacrifice: Years ago, Asriel found the first human (Chara) and became their best friend. When Chara died, Asriel absorbed their soul to cross the barrier and return their body to the surface, but he refused to fight back when humans attacked him.
  • The Rebirth: He collapsed and died in his father's garden, his dust spreading over the flowers. Later, he was accidentally "resurrected" as Flowey through Alphys's experiments with Determination.

Trivia

  • Name Origin: "Asriel" is a combination of his parents' names (**As**gore and To**riel**). It is also phonetically similar to "Azrael," the Angel of Death in some mythologies.
  • Final Reflection: After the final battle, Asriel can be found at the very beginning of the game, standing in the bed of golden flowers where the protagonist first fell.
  • Memory: He is the only character who remembers the events of the True Pacifist ending even after the protagonist leaves the Underground, urging the player to let Frisk live their life.
