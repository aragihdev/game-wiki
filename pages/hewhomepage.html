New Home

The capital of the Underground and the final residential area before reaching the Barrier.

New Home is the mirror image of the Ruins, but rendered in grayscale and white tones. It represents the pinnacle of monster civilization, built after they migrated from their original home. This is where Frisk finally reaches the palace of Asgore, walking through the history of the fallen kingdom while being told the story of Asriel and Chara by the monsters they met along the way.

Geographical Characteristics

The aesthetic of New Home is monochromatic and somber. While it shares the same architectural layout as the Ruins, it feels vast and empty. The capital is a sprawling city of white buildings, but the player only traverses the "Last Corridor" and the King's home, which is filled with golden flowers—a stark contrast to the gray surroundings.

Key Locations

  • Asgore's House: A near-perfect replica of Toriel's home in the Ruins, though it feels colder and more formal.
  • The Last Corridor (Judgment Hall): A golden, sun-drenched hallway where Sans evaluates the protagonist's actions throughout the game.
  • The Throne Room: A massive chamber filled with golden flowers where Asgore waits, tending to his garden.
  • The Coffin Room: A basement area containing seven coffins intended for the human souls, including one with a red heart for Chara.
  • The Barrier: The magical seal that traps the monsters underground, located just past the throne room.

Inhabitants and Monsters

New Home is mostly empty of hostile encounters, serving as a narrative climax.

The Judge: Sans stands in the Last Corridor to judge the player's EXP (Execution Points) and LV (Level of Violence).

The King: Asgore Dreemurr is found here, offering tea and preparing for the final confrontation.

Narrative Spirits: In the Neutral/Pacifist routes, various monsters appear as silhouettes to tell the story of the first human (Chara) and the tragedy of the royal family.

Gameplay Mechanics

This area replaces traditional combat puzzles with narrative weight and moral reflection:

  • Judgment: The player is confronted with the consequences of every monster they have killed or spared.
  • Storytelling: Interaction with objects in Asgore's house provides deep lore about the Dreemurr family.
  • The Final Choice: The player must decide whether to FIGHT or SPARE the King, knowing that only one soul can leave through the barrier.

Impact on Routes

The journey ends where it truly began:

Pacifist/Neutral Route: The monsters speak of hope and the tragedy of Asriel. If the player reaches the end of the True Pacifist path, this location leads to the final battle against the "God of Hyperdeath."

Genocide Route: The storytelling is replaced by Flowey's confession and Chara's internal monologue. The "Judgment" becomes the hardest boss fight in the game against Sans, who attempts to stop the player from erasing the world.
