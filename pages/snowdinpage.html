Snowdin

It is the first major region the player explores after leaving the Ruins, characterized by its perpetual winter and eccentric inhabitants.

Snowdin is a vast, snowy forest that leads into a cozy, rustic town. It serves as the primary home for many monsters, including the skeleton brothers Sans and Papyrus. Unlike the silent and isolated Ruins, Snowdin is bustling with life, shops, and social activity, representing the protagonist's first true step into the wider culture of the Underground.

Geographical Characteristics

The region is defined by a cold, pine-filled forest environment and a town with a "holiday" aesthetic. Despite being deep underground, the area is constantly covered in snow and ice. The architecture in the town is primarily made of wood, giving it a warm, inviting contrast to the freezing temperatures of the forest.

Key Locations

  • The Box Road: A series of puzzles and sentry stations managed by Sans and Papyrus to intercept humans.
  • Snowdin Town: The heart of the region, featuring a shop, an inn, and several residential buildings.
  • Grillby's: A popular pub and the favorite hangout spot for Sans and many local monsters.
  • The Library (Librarby): A small building filled with books that provide deep lore about monster history and soul mechanics.
  • Skelbro House: The iconic residence of Sans and Papyrus, containing many interactive objects that reflect their personalities.
  • The Greater Dog's Post: One of the many sentry stations occupied by the members of the Royal Guard.

Inhabitants and Monsters

Snowdin is protected by several members of the Royal Guard and is home to many unique NPCs.

NPCs: The brothers Sans and Papyrus are the most prominent figures. Players also meet the shopkeeper, the innkeeper, and Monster Kid, who looks up to Undyne.

The Royal Guard: The "Dogi" units patrol this area, including Doggo (who only sees movement), Dogamy and Dogaressa, Lesser Dog, and Greater Dog.

Random Encounters: Include Snowdrake (the pun-loving bird), Ice Cap (who is very proud of his hat), Jerry (whom everyone dislikes), and Gyftrot.

Gameplay Mechanics

This area introduces more complex interaction mechanics and environmental hazards:

  • Blue Attacks: Introduced by Papyrus and Doggo, requiring the player to stay perfectly still to avoid damage.
  • Ice Physics: Several puzzles and pathways feature slippery ice that affects the protagonist's movement.
  • Complex Puzzles: Puzzles involving sliding tiles, invisible mazes, and timing-based challenges created by Papyrus.

Impact on Routes

Snowdin serves as a turning point for the player's reputation:

Pacifist/Neutral Route: If the player befriends Papyrus, they can hang out or go on a "date" at his house, which is a requirement for the True Pacifist ending.

Genocide Route: The town is completely evacuated. The shopkeeper leaves a note, and the protagonist can steal items and gold. The upbeat music is replaced by a distorted, slow ambient track, and Papyrus attempts to redeem the human with a "blue" hug before his inevitable confrontation.
