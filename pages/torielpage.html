Toriel

Guardian of the Ruins and Former Queen of the Underground

Toriel is the second character the protagonist encounters in Undertale. She rescues the human from Flowey and guides them through the Ruins, acting as a protective mother figure who wishes to keep the child safe from the dangers of the kingdom.

Physical Characteristics: A Boss Monster with the appearance of a white, anthropomorphic nubian goat. She has drooping ears, small horns, and wears a long purple robe (a duster) adorned with the Delta Rune.

Personality

Toriel is a kind and maternal individual who genuinely cares for the well-being of the humans who fall into her home:

  • Nurturing: She enjoys cooking, especially baking Butterscotch-Cinnamon pie, and has a strong desire to be a mother and a teacher.

  • Protective: Her kindness is often overshadowed by her fear of losing another child, which can make her stubborn or overprotective, leading her to block the exit of the Ruins.
  • Humor: Despite her serious role, she has a secret love for puns and bad jokes, which helped her form a bond with Sans through the door of the Ruins.

Abilities and Powers

As a Boss Monster, Toriel is exceptionally powerful, though she uses her magic primarily for protection or gentle guidance:

  • Fire Magic: She can summon waves of fireballs to attack or defend. In battle, her fireballs will actively avoid the protagonist if they are low on health.
  • Healing: She possesses knowledge of healing magic and basic first aid, which she uses to tend to the protagonist after their encounter with Flowey.
  • Boss Monster Soul: Her soul is strong enough to persist briefly after death, a trait unique to her species (and Asgore).

History

Before the events of the game, Toriel was the Queen of the Underground and the wife of King Asgore.

  • The Tragedy: After the death of her children, Asriel and Chara, she became disgusted by Asgore's decision to declare war on humanity and collect seven human souls.
  • Exile: She abdicated her throne and moved to the Ruins, where she took it upon herself to care for any human children who fell through the barrier, hoping to prevent their inevitable fate.

Trivia

  • Name Origin: Her name, "Toriel," is a play on the word "Tutorial," reflecting her role as the game's guide.
  • Journal: She keeps a diary of bad jokes, which suggests she has been practicing her comedy for a long time.
  • Snail Facts: Toriel is remarkably fond of snails and often reads books about them, even considering "Snail Pie" as a culinary option.
