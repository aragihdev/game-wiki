Hotland

The industrial heart of the Underground, a high-tech volcanic region filled with steam vents, lasers, and complex machinery.

Hotland is a stark contrast to the previous regions, replacing nature with advanced technology and industrial infrastructure. It is the location of the Royal Lab and the massive CORE. Throughout this area, Frisk is constantly monitored by Alphys and "hunted" by the Underground's greatest star, Mettaton, in a series of televised death traps.

Geographical Characteristics

The region is characterized by orange and red hues, rivers of lava, and a constant haze of heat. It is divided into three main floors, connected by high-speed elevators. The landscape is dominated by artificial structures, including conveyor belts, cooling systems, and massive television monitors advertising Mettaton's brand.

Key Locations

  • The Royal Lab: The home and workplace of Alphys, filled with monitors, anime posters, and a hidden entrance to the True Lab.
  • MTT Resort: A luxury hotel and restaurant complex owned by Mettaton, serving as a social hub before the entrance to the CORE.
  • The CORE: A massive technological facility that converts geothermal energy into magical electricity for the entire Underground.
  • Muffet's Lair: A spider-web-filled cavern where the protagonist must survive a "bake sale" confrontation.
  • Elevators: The primary mode of transport between the different levels of the volcanic terrain.

Inhabitants and Monsters

Hotland is populated by tech-savvy monsters and the specialized Royal Guard units.

NPCs: Alphys acts as a nervous guide via phone calls, while Mettaton interrupts with various TV show segments. Sans can also be found here selling hot dogs.

The Royal Guard: The duo RG 01 and RG 02 patrol the area, often found guarding the path to the CORE.

Random Encounters: Include Vulkin (the misguided volcano), Tsunderplane (the airplane monster), Pyrope, and So Sorry.

Gameplay Mechanics

Hotland features the most complex environmental puzzles in the game:

  • Laser Puzzles: Requiring the player to move or stop based on blue and orange laser colors.
  • Steam Vents: Used as jump pads to navigate between platforms in a specific sequence.
  • Jetpack/Phone Upgrades: Alphys upgrades the protagonist's phone with several "apps" that help solve puzzles and fight Mettaton.

Impact on Routes

The journey through the heat tests the player's resolve:

Pacifist/Neutral Route: The player participates in Mettaton's finale, focusing on ratings rather than damage. This route also opens the path to the True Lab later on.

Genocide Route: The region is eerily quiet as most monsters have evacuated to the CORE. The puzzles are often pre-solved or disabled, and the tension culminates in a swift confrontation with Mettaton NEO.
