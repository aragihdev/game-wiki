Flowey

The Prince in a Heartless Form

Flowey is the first character the protagonist encounters in Undertale. He serves as the game's primary antagonist, acting as a dark foil to the player's ability to save and reset the world.

Physical Characteristics: A golden flower with six yellow petals, a white center, and a highly expressive face capable of horrifying distortions.

Personality

Flowey is defined by his nihilism and lack of empathy. Because he lacks a soul, he is unable to feel love, compassion, or joy.

  • Philosophical Outlook: He operates under the mantra: "In this world, it's kill or be killed."
  • The Boredom of Power: Having used the power of SAVE and LOAD countless times before Frisk's arrival, Flowey has seen every possible outcome in the Underground, leading to a deep, twisted boredom that fuels his cruelty.
  • Deceptive Nature: He initially presents himself as a "friend" to lure the protagonist into a false sense of security before attempting to harvest their soul.

Abilities and Powers

Despite his fragile appearance, Flowey is one of the most dangerous entities in the Underground:

  • Friendliness Pellets: White, seed-like bullets that he uses to attack.
  • Save-File Manipulation In certain routes, Flowey can "crash" the game or overwrite the player's save data, demonstrating meta-awareness of the game's mechanics.
  • Vines and Face-Shifting: He can manifest thick, thorny vines to trap opponents and can mimic the faces and voices of other characters to taunt the player.
  • Omega Flowey (Photoshop Flowey): Upon absorbing the six human souls, he transforms into a gargantuan, eldritch machine-like being with near-infinite power over the game's reality.

History

Flowey was created in Alphys's Lab as part of an experiment with Determination.

  • The Vessel: Alphys injected Determination into the first golden flower to bloom in the Garden ( the same flower that grew where Asriel Dreemurr died ).
  • The Reawakening: Without a soul to ground him, Asriel's memories returned within the flower, but his ability to feel emotions did not. After attempting to love his parents and failing, he realized his new existence was a hollow one, eventually turning to malice out of curiosity.

Trivia

  • Voice: He is one of the few characters with a voice clip ("That's a wonderful idea!").
  • Fourth Wall: Flowey is the only character who consistently acknowledges the player as a separate entity from the protagonist.
  • Echo Flowers: He is often mistaken for an Echo Flower by other monsters, allowing him to travel through the Underground unnoticed.
